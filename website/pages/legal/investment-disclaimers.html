2. Real Estate Investment Risks

Warning: Real estate investments are subject to numerous risks that could result in partial or total loss of your investment.

2.1 Market Risk

Real estate values can fluctuate significantly due to:

  • Economic conditions and market cycles
  • Interest rate changes
  • Supply and demand imbalances
  • Local market conditions
  • Neighborhood changes and development

2.2 Liquidity Risk

Real estate is generally illiquid, meaning:

  • Properties may take months or years to sell
  • You may be forced to sell at unfavorable prices
  • Market conditions may prevent sale entirely
  • Transaction costs can be substantial

2.3 Property-Specific Risks

  • Physical Condition: Hidden defects, structural issues, environmental hazards
  • Legal Issues: Title problems, liens, zoning violations, code violations
  • Rental Properties: Vacancy, tenant damage, collection issues, maintenance costs
  • Natural Disasters: Floods, earthquakes, hurricanes, fires

2.4 Financial Risks

  • Interest rate increases affecting financing costs
  • Property tax increases
  • Insurance cost increases or coverage denial
  • Unexpected repair and maintenance expenses
  • Cash flow shortfalls
